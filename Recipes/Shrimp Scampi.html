Shrimp Scampi with Capellini Pasta

Shrimp scampi in the lemon-wine sauce served on top of capellini or angel hair pasta will be the best summer pasta you ever had. The kick of chilli flakes complements the shrimp and the lemony sauce perfectly!

Ingredients

Serves 1

  • 8-12 shrimp, peeled and de-veined
  • 4 oz dry capellini / angelhair pasta
  • 2 Tbsp Extra Virgin Olive oil
  • 3 each garlic bulbs
  • 1 each large shallot, small diced
  • 2 tsp chili flakes
  • 2 each lemons
  • ¼ cup white wine
  • 2 Tbsp capers
  • ½ Cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ Cup seafood stock
  • 2 oz butter, cubed
  • 1 parmesan cheese wedge
  • 1 sprig basil, picked
  • TT Salt and Fresh Cracked Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Add water to a 4 quart sauce pot. Salt liberally, cover with a lid and bring to a boil.
  2. Heat a medium nonstick saute pan on medium heat with one tablespoon of olive oil.
  3. When hot, add the diced shallots and finely grate the garlic bulbs into the pan and saute for 2 minutes to soften them.
  4. Deglaze the pan with the white wine and cook until it is almost evaporated from the pan.
  5. Add the seafood stock, chili flakes, capers, cherry tomatoes and lemons (zested and juiced).
  6. When the sauce comes to a simmer, season the raw shrimp with salt and pepper and poach in the liquid for about 1 minute per side.
  7. Add the cubed butter to melt.
  8. Taste and season the sauce accordingly.
  9. Meanwhile, when the pot of water begins to boil, add the pasta and use tongs or a fork to gradually push it into the seasoned water as it starts to soften. Boil the pasta, stirring occasionally so it doesn’t stick together, for about 3-5 minutes, or until al dente.
  10. Strain the pasta and quickly add it to the pan with the scampi sauce and cooked shrimp. Toss the pasta in the sauce to coat evenly.
  11. Finally, tear the fresh basil leaves and toss into the pasta before plating.
  12. Plate the cooked pasta into a medium size bowl with some height. Garnish with a grating of Parmesan cheese.
